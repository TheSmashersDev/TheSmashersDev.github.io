My name is Jackson Clayton. I’ve been making games since the fourth grade. I’m obsessed with 3D engines and level design. I design games that people indirectly teach the player a skill and test them on it throughout the game all using level design.

Language Fluent

While I have spent most of my time coding in C#, I am also fluent in Java. I have previous experience with Lua (Pico-8), Python, netbeans, and C.
I have thousands of hours in Unity and Scratch, and have experience in GameMaker and Unreal.

Beyond Game Development

I was the lead of documentation in my High School's robotics team. I was also a reporter and cartoonist for my high school's magazine and have lots of experience in Adobe inDesign and Illustrator.

Modder at Heart

I started game development and level design through mod-tools. I have experience with the hammer editor in both Quake 1 and Counter Strike Global Offensive.
I worked on an overhaul mod of the Quake mod NZP in the FTE source port of Quake 1.


I put a lot of effort into making and editing quality trailers for my newly released games. Making trailers is one of my favorite parts of releasing a game. I will spend hours recording clips to use, and then run dozens of iterations by friends until I can get the best flowing trailer. Watching the playlist you can see my skills have gone up drastically over time. I have recently improved with pacing and getting better at syncing clips with the trailer music.
